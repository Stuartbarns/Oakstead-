Our Story

Built by the communities it serves.

Oakstead Light Foundation started with a simple observation: young people and families across West Nile's rural districts had little safe, judgment-free access to sexual and reproductive health information, or to mental health support after crisis. We set out to close that gap — not with clinics from the outside, but with trained local volunteers who already have the community's trust.

Today, our teams run outreach sessions, peer counseling, and school-based education programs across Zombo, Pakwachi, and Nebbi — funded almost entirely by individual donors and supported by a growing volunteer network.

Our Vision

"A society where every individual enjoys optimal mental well-being, exercises their sexual and reproductive rights freely, and lives a healthy, dignified, and empowered life."

Our Mission

"To promote mental health and sexual and reproductive health and rights through education, advocacy, research, community engagement, and access to quality support services, especially for vulnerable populations."

Our Objectives

What we're working toward.

Our general objective is to improve the mental health and sexual and reproductive well-being of individuals and communities through awareness, empowerment, advocacy, and access to essential services.

1

Promote mental health awareness and reduce stigma associated with mental illness.

2

Improve access to mental health support and counseling services.

3

Advance sexual and reproductive health education and awareness.

4

Advocate for the protection and promotion of sexual and reproductive health rights.

5

Empower young people, women, and vulnerable groups to make informed health decisions.

6

Strengthen community participation in health promotion and disease prevention.
